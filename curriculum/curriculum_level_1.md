# Level 1 Curriculum Overview (Ages 8–10)

Goal: Introduce children to the core structures that govern reality and build curiosity about how the world works.

## Part I — Patterns (Lessons 1–10)

1. Patterns Everywhere
2. Finding the Rule
3. Patterns in Nature
4. Patterns in Numbers
5. Patterns in Behavior
6. Sorting and Classification
7. Categories and Boundaries
8. Analogies: Seeing Similarity
9. Hidden Patterns
10. When Patterns Mislead

## Part II — Cause and Effect (Lessons 11–20)

11. What Caused This?
12. Coincidence vs Cause
13. Testing an Idea
14. Changing One Thing
15. Experiments
16. Mechanisms: How Things Work
17. False Causes
18. Multiple Causes
19. Chain Reactions
20. Predicting Effects

## Part III — Chance and Uncertainty (Lessons 21–30)

21. Randomness
22. Coin Flips
23. Dice and Probability
24. Predicting Outcomes
25. Good Guesses
26. Better vs Worse Bets
27. Why Some Events Are Unpredictable
28. Patterns vs Randomness
29. When Luck Matters
30. Thinking with Uncertainty

## Part IV — Models and Explanations (Lessons 31–40)

31. Maps and Territories
32. Why Maps Simplify
33. Drawing a Model
34. Assumptions
35. Testing a Model
36. Improving a Model
37. Different Models for the Same Thing
38. When Models Fail
39. Models of Human Behavior
40. Using Models to Predict

## Part V — Decisions and Consequences (Lessons 41–52)

41. Making Choices
42. Tradeoffs
43. Opportunity Cost
44. Incentives
45. Rewards and Punishments
46. Planning Ahead
47. Short‑Term vs Long‑Term Thinking
48. Unintended Consequences
49. Decisions Under Uncertainty
50. Learning from Mistakes
51. Improving Decisions
52. Thinking Like a Scientist

## Capstone Project

Students investigate a real‑world question by identifying patterns, proposing causes, testing ideas, and revising explanations.
