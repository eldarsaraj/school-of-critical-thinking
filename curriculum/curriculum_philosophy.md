# The School of Critical Thinking — Homeschool Curriculum Project Plan

Motto: Teaching children how the world actually works.
Core Idea: Most education teaches children facts. Very little education teaches children how reality actually behaves.

Children are rarely taught:

- how causes work
- how probability works
- how incentives shape behavior
- how models represent reality
- how decisions involve tradeoffs

The School of Critical Thinking curriculum teaches the structures that govern reality.

The central principle is that critical thinking means aligning the structures in your mind with the structures of the world.

## Curriculum Architecture

The curriculum consists of three progressive levels.

- Level 1 (Ages 8–10): Discovering how the world works
- Level 2 (Ages 11–13): Understanding systems and reasoning
- Level 3 (Ages 14–16): Applying thinking to real decisions

Each level contains 52 short lessons designed to be taught once per week. Each lesson takes about 20–30 minutes.

### Five Structures of Reality

1. Patterns — recognizing structure in the world
2. Causality — understanding cause and effect
3. Uncertainty — reasoning under probability
4. Models — representations that simplify reality
5. Decisions — incentives, tradeoffs, and consequences

### Teaching Method

Each lesson includes:

1. A story or scenario
2. A thinking challenge
3. Discussion questions
4. A real‑world application

The goal is exploration and reasoning rather than memorization.

Almost no existing “critical thinking for kids” curriculum teaches this.

Our framework instead teaches:

- how the world actually works
