Teaching children how the world actually works.

Most curricula teach children facts and skills. Almost none teach them how reality behaves: how causes work, how probability affects decisions, how decisions involve tradeoffs.

The School of Critical Thinking aims to change that.

A quick test

Can your child explain the difference between:

  • A coincidence and a cause?
  • A good guess and a lucky one?
  • A rule and an exception?

These aren't trick questions. They're the foundations of clear thinking, and almost no curriculum teaches them.

Most people believe that critical thinking is about riddles or logic puzzles. But, it's not. It is about understanding the structures that govern reality: how patterns form, how causes produce effects, how uncertainty should be reasoned about, how models simplify the world, and how decisions involve tradeoffs.

A child who understands these structures doesn't just know more — they think differently. They ask better questions, spot weak reasoning, and make decisions that hold up under pressure.

What we offer

The School provides a structured curriculum for homeschooling families, built around five core structures of reality. Three progressive levels take children from ages 8 through 16, with 52 lessons per level — one per week, 20–30 minutes each.

  • Curriculum and lesson materials — structured, ready to teach, no prior knowledge required
  • Books — written companions to each level of the curriculum
  • Live instruction — sessions taught directly by the School's founder
  • Consultation — for parents who want guidance on teaching critical thinking at home
