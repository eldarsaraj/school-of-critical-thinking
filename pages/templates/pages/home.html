Teaching children how the world actually works.

If you are a parent, you want the best for your kids. You want them to grow up smart, capable, and ready to take on the world. You want them to be able to think clearly, make good decisions, and navigate a complex world with confidence.

But, how to do that?

What should you teach them? How do you prepare them for a future that is uncertain and rapidly changing?

Both public and private school curricula emphasize facts and memorization. If you're lucky enough to be homeschooling, you have more freedom, but you still have to figure out what to teach and how to teach it. Almost none of the existing curricula teach children about how the world really works: how patterns permeate reality, how causes produce effects, how explanations simplify (and sometimes distort) the world, and how decisions involve tradeoffs.

The School of Critical Thinking aims to change that.

A quick test

Can your child explain the difference between:

  • A coincidence and a cause?
  • A good guess and a lucky one?
  • A rule and an exception?

These aren't trick questions. They're the foundations of clear thinking, and almost no curriculum teaches them.

Most people believe that critical thinking is about riddles or logic puzzles. But, it's not. It is about understanding the structures that govern reality: patterns, causes, randomness, and complexity. These may sound complex, but they're not. They are learnable, intuitive skills most eight-year-olds can grasp.

A child who understands these structures will grow up to think differently. They will ask better questions, spot weak reasoning, and make decisions that hold up under pressure.

What we offer

The School provides a structured curriculum for families that take charge of their children's education in thinking and reasoning. The curriculum is built around five core structures of reality. Three progressive levels (with three separate modules each) take children from ages 8 to 16 through a series of structured lessons that teach them how reality really works. The syllabus is free of ideology, controversy and politics. Our aim is to help your child understand the world as it is, not as we wish it were. It is designed (by a parent) to be taught by other parents, with no specialist background required.

  • Curriculum and lesson materials — structured, ready to teach, no prior knowledge required
  • Books — written companions to each level of the curriculum
  • Live instruction — sessions taught directly by the School's founder
  • Consultation — for parents who want guidance on teaching critical thinking at home
