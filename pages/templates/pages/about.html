About the School

The School of Critical Thinking was founded on a simple observation: education teaches children what to think, but almost never teaches them how reality actually works.

The gap

Children are taught facts, skills, and subject knowledge. They are rarely taught how causes work, how probability behaves, how models represent reality, or how decisions involve tradeoffs. These are not advanced topics — they are the foundations of clear thinking, and they can be taught to an eight-year-old.

Most "critical thinking" curricula fill this gap with riddles, logic puzzles, and lists of fallacies. These are not useless, but they are not the same as teaching children how the world actually works. That is what this School does.

The framework

The curriculum is built around five structures of reality that govern how the world behaves:

  • Patterns — recognizing structure in the world
  • Causality — understanding cause and effect
  • Uncertainty — reasoning under probability
  • Models — how representations simplify reality
  • Decisions — incentives, tradeoffs, and consequences

A child who understands these structures has a mental model of how reality behaves. That is the foundation of good thinking in any subject, and in life.

Who it is for

The School's primary focus is homeschooling families. The curriculum is designed to be taught by parents — no specialist background required. Three progressive levels take children from ages 8 through 16, with 52 lessons per level, each 20–30 minutes long.

The School also works with adults. The Thinking Diagnostic is a self-assessment tool for parents and professionals who want to identify their own reasoning blindspots and receive a personalized improvement plan. Consultation and live instruction are available for both children and adults.

What we provide

  • Curriculum and lesson materials for homeschooling families
  • Books written as companions to each curriculum level
  • Live instruction taught directly by the School's founder
  • Consultation for parents teaching critical thinking at home
  • The Thinking Diagnostic — a free self-assessment for adults

Founder perspective

I built this School out of a practical frustration. Formal education — at every level — teaches content but not thinking. It teaches what is known, but not how to reason about what isn't known yet. It teaches subjects, but not the structures that underlie all subjects. I have a daughter, and I realized that if I wanted her to learn how to think, I would have to teach her myself. The School of Critical Thinking is the result of that realization. It is the curriculum I wish I had when I was a child, and the one I use to help my daughter become a better thinker and thrive in our uncertain and ever-changing world.

My background is in philosophy. Philosophy is useful here because it forces first-principles thinking about what thinking actually is. But the School is not a philosophy class. The goal is practical: to give children — and adults — the mental tools to reason clearly, decide well, and navigate a world that does not come with instructions.

— Eldar Sarajlic
Founder, The School of Critical Thinking
