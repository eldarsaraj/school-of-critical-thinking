About the School

The School of Critical Thinking was founded on a simple observation: education teaches children what to think, but almost never teaches them how reality actually works.

The gap

Children are taught facts, skills, and subject knowledge. They are rarely taught how to distinguish causes from coincidences, how uncertainties behave, how models represent reality, or how decisions involve tradeoffs. They are often left to figure these things out on their own, once they leave school and grow up. That's a wasted potential. Children should learn these things early, when their minds are most flexible and open to new ideas. Doing that would give them advantages in every subject they study, and in every decision they will ever make. These are not advanced topics. They are the foundations of clear thinking, and they can be taught to an eight-year-old.

Most "critical thinking" curricula fill this gap with riddles, logic puzzles, and lists of fallacies. These are not useless, but they are not the same as teaching children how the world actually works.

That is what this School does.

The framework

The curriculum is built around five structures of reality that govern how the world behaves:

  • Patterns — recognizing structure in the world
  • Causality — understanding cause and effect
  • Uncertainty — reasoning under probability
  • Models — how representations simplify reality
  • Decisions — incentives, tradeoffs, and consequences

A child who understands these structures has a mental model of how reality behaves. That is the foundation of good thinking in any subject, and in life.

Who it is for

The School's primary focus is homeschooling families. The curriculum is designed to be taught by parents — no specialist background required. Three progressive levels take children from ages 8 through 16, with 52 lessons per level, each 20–30 minutes long.

The School also works with adults. The Thinking Diagnostic is a self-assessment tool for parents and professionals who want to identify their own reasoning blindspots and receive a personalized improvement plan. Consultation and live instruction are available for both children and adults.

What we provide

  • Curriculum and lesson materials for homeschooling families
  • Books written as companions to each curriculum level
  • Live instruction taught directly by the School's founder
  • Consultation for parents teaching critical thinking at home
  • The Thinking Diagnostic — a free self-assessment for adults

Founder perspective

I have spent years studying how people think — and how education shapes, or fails to shape, that capacity. My academic work in philosophy of education led me to a conclusion that is hard to argue with once you see it: formal schooling teaches children what to think, almost never how. It delivers facts, skills, and subject knowledge, but leaves the underlying structures of good reasoning entirely untouched.

That conclusion became personal when my daughter started school. Watching her move through a curriculum that was rich in content and thin on thinking confirmed everything I had studied. If I wanted her to learn how to reason clearly — about causes, about uncertainty, about tradeoffs — I would have to teach her myself. The School of Critical Thinking grew out of that effort. It is the curriculum I wish had existed, built on years of research and refined through the most honest test I know: teaching my own child.

The goal is practical, not academic. Philosophy is useful here because it forces first-principles thinking about what thinking actually is. But this is not a philosophy class. It is a structured way to give children — and adults — the mental tools to reason well and navigate a world that does not come with instructions.

— Eldar Sarajlic
Founder, The School of Critical Thinking
